Exploring the NOOA storm database: A temporal analysis of human casualties and property damage in 10 U.S cities

Synopsis

This project involves exploring the U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administrations (NOAA) storm database. The results were affected by the large amount of fields that were outside of the specification adopted by the NOOA. Furthermore, it is possible have an ideia of the impact, even underestimated, in the human and property damages. It is possible observes that the Tornado event causes more damages, fatalities and injuries, that the other events. Moreover, the events that caused more casualties are concentred in the Texas and Illinois states. It is also observes, that the Flood and Tornado events produces the greatest economic loss in relation to the other events.
